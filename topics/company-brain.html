The definition

        

          

            
A company brain is the organized memory a business keeps so AI agents can do real work: contacts, calls, decisions, brand voice, pricing rules, project history, and the permissions that govern what an agent may read or change. It is not a document dump. The difference between a folder of files and a company brain is structure — required fields, sources of truth, freshness rules, and a review gate before anything writes back.

            
Shoreline cares about this topic because it is the spine of the whole show. Episode 1 builds the first version out of Notion, CRM records, and agent lanes. Episode 2 turns it into an operations layer that a sales rep can talk to from plane Wi-Fi. Episode 3 asks whether a big enough company brain ends app switching entirely. Episode 4 reframes it as the constraint on everything: AI falls flat on hard tasks not because the model is weak, but because it is missing the context of your life and work.

Open questionsStill being argued

        

          

            Question 01
            
One universal agent, or many narrow ones?

            
Episode 3 frames the fork: as context windows grow, does the company brain feed a single agent that moves across support, engineering, and operations — or does review burden keep agents in narrow lanes?

          

          

            Question 02
            
How fresh does memory have to be?

            
Old strategy docs and stale customer notes quietly poison an agent. The show's freshness rule is easy to state and hard to operate: who owns expiring the brain's bad memories?

          

          

            Question 03
            
Where does the soft context live?

            
Episode 4 points at everything that never gets written down — calls, reading, in-person conversations. Capturing it is the highest-leverage gap, and nobody has a clean answer yet.

          

          

            Question 04
            
Who reviews the brain's writes?

            
From wiped CRM tags in Episode 1 to bulk edits at scale: as more agents write to shared memory, the human approval gate is still the part of the system nobody has automated away.
