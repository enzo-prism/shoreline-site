The gist

        

          

            
Episode 3 is the bridge between technical AI tooling and the cultural shift around AI-native work. When context windows get larger and token spend gets cheaper relative to human time, the limiting factor shifts from "can the model answer?" to "did we give it the right company memory and review surface?"

            
The starter notes keep the page focused on verified episode themes: token maxing, company brain workflows, Codex vs Claude Code vs Cursor, model routing, OpenRouter, and the idea that apps may become less important than the operating layer around them.

Theme map

        

          

            01
            

              
Token maxing

              
Spend more context and model effort when the output creates reviewed business value. Tokens become working capital.

            

          

          

            02
            

              
Company brain era

              
The best agent workflows depend on company memory that is organized, searchable, current, and safe to act on.

            

          

          

            03
            

              
Tool choice is workflow fit.

              
Codex, Claude Code, Cursor, and related tools should be judged by context fit, interface, review burden, and output quality.

            

          

          

            04
            

              
The app layer gets thinner.

              
When agents can read, remember, and route work, the product surface starts to look more like an operating layer than a traditional app.

            

          

        

      

    


    

      

        
Operator playbookTokens as working capital

        

          

            Step 01
            
Classify the task.

            
Use cheaper or local models for drafts and extraction. Use frontier models for architecture, judgment, hard debugging, and final review.

          

          

            Step 02
            
Route by risk.

            
Low-risk exploration can burn more tokens. Customer-facing, revenue, or database changes need slower review and stronger logs.

          

          

            Step 03
            
Preserve context.

            
Write down what the agent learned, where output landed, and what should be reused next time.

          

          

            Step 04
            
Compare tools by workflow fit.

            
Test Codex, Claude Code, Cursor, and other tools on the same task. Keep the one that creates the least review burden.

Router
Best when model choice is the experiment
